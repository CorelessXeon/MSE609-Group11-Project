STUDY INFORMATION

1. Keywords: Digital Health, Virtual Care, e-Mental Health, Patient Access, Health Information, Connected Care

2. Abstract: The Canadian Digital Health Survey is a series of surveys conducted annually commissioned by Canada Health Infoway (Infoway) through a third-party vendor. These annually conducted surveys are based on a variety of specific system utilization and attitudinal tracking metrics used in previous years as well as new areas of inquiry. The surveys include core tracking questions as well as new questions that will provide actionable insights to help advance the digital health landscape in Canada. For visualization and be able to filter data to gain insights into the Canadian digital health landscape, please visit Infoway's interactive data and analytics hub: https://insights.infoway-inforoute.ca/ (2025-07-14) 

3. Data collection: 15-20 minute online survey conducted February 6th – March 10th, 2025. Web survey of 12,153 citizens in Canada over the age of 16. The survey was made available in both English and French. Using Canadian Census data, weighting was applied according to region, age, and gender in order to render a representative sample of the Canadian population. Sample base sizes indicated are unweighted. A margin of error cannot be associated with a non-probability sample in a panel survey. For comparison purposes, a probability sample of this size would have a margin of error ±0.889%, 19 times out of 20. Ethics approval was obtained before fielding the survey. The ethics approval was granted by an independent institutional review board in Jan. 2025, Veritas IRB Inc (IRB Tracking Number: 2025-3661-20237-4). In addition, the results presented in this study comply with the public opinion research standards and disclosure requirements of CRIC (the Canadian research and insights council) and the global ESOMAR network.
